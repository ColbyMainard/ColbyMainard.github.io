Quantum Computing

This area of nascent technology appears unapproachable, the easiest way to visualize this would be rotating a circle, sphere, or hypersphere (ball in 4+ dimensions) in such a way that its north pole is pointing towards the correct answer. Each qubit, in this case, would keep track of the rotation of data in this space. The YouTube channel 3Blue1Brown has an excellent brief explainer here.

While the list of widely-available algorithms for quantum computers are somewhat limited, I was able to teach myself to a reasonably-fine-grain level with Quantum Computing: An Applied Approach by Jack D. Hidary and Programming Quantum Computers: Essential Algorithms and Code Samples by Eric R. Johnston, Nic Harrigan, and Mercedes Gimeno-Segovia.

History

Why

I have two main reasons that drew me into history: academic and curiosity.

"History doesnâ€™t repeat itself, but it does rhyme." - Mark Twain

Academically, it is interesting to know how historical events decades or centuries ago still have ripples impacting the world today. While history isn't a perfect representation of current events, it does allow us to figure out trends we should expect to see. Many would think that concerns around automation negatively impacting the labor market is a uniquely 21st century problem, but the Luddites had similar concerns around automated looms. The Luddite movement actually was the origin of the word sabotage.

My curiosity was then intrigued by people and events that tend to buck the historical trends that we would expect for that time and place. While understanding trends is good to understand what to expect going forward, it is also healthy to be curious about when those trends do not hold. These unusual events could have a number of potential components making them more complicated, like:

  • Supernatural: Casket girls, immigrant girls who could be the first "vampires" in the United States.
  • Comical: trading onion futures becoming illegal due to the "Onion King".
  • Ironic: Catholic Church declares war on cats, making the plague worse.
  • Seemingly-anachronistic: Suitcases got wheels after humanity landed on the moon.
