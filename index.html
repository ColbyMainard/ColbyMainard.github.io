Designed and built machine learning models as part of a five-person team that were able to diagnose a patient with COVID-19 by examining chest X-ray data. Specifically, I was responsible for designing, training, and validating two neural networks. The better model had 94% accuracy in predicting whether the patient tested positive for COVID-19. Autonomous Vehicle | Testing | Spring 2020

As part of a continuing Texas A&M AutoDrive team, I was tasked with expanding the virtual testing suite to encompass real-world scenarios. The task entailed converting any arbitrary map in HD LiveMap (HDLM) format to an equivalent Unreal Engine 4 (UE4) map where simulations could be run.

Steganography | Research Project | Summer 2019

Under direction of Dr. Ritchey, Texas A&M, extended research into hide-in-plain-sight (steganography) techniques (using Sudoku puzzles). The hypothesis tested is that steganography combined with cryptography may offer a powerful secondary approach for certain cybersecurity applications.

Quantum Computing

This area of nascent technology appears unapproachable, the easiest way to visualize this would be rotating a circle, sphere, or hypersphere (ball in 4+ dimensions) in such a way that its north pole is pointing towards the only correct. Each qubit, in this case, would keep track of the rotation of data in this space. The YouTube channel 3Blue1Brown has an excellent brief explainer here.

While the list of widely-available algorithms for quantum computers are somewhat limited, I was able to teach myself to a reasonably-fine-grain level with Quantum Computing: An Applied Approach by Jack D. Hidary and Programming Quantum Computers: Essential Algorithms and Code Samples by Eric R. Johnston, Nic Harrigan, and Mercedes Gimeno-Segovia.
